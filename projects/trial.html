THE TRIAL (deconstructed)

Book Design
Typography
Book-binding


Responding to the 2025 ISTD Brief "Books Still"

This project explores contemporary reading practices by deliberately juxtaposing traditional book forms with the digital vernacular of web browsing, hyperlinks, and social media interactions. Franz Kafka’s The Trial serves as an ideal text for this exploration due to its thematic resonance with the modern experience of navigating digital spaces—particularly the internet’s inherent confusion, fragmentation, and pervasive sense of unanswered questions.

The target audience comprises digitally native readers familiar with navigating layered and fragmented content. By physically embodying the behaviors characteristic of digital reading within the tactile space of the printed page, this design concept encourages critical reflection on how contemporary media shapes our reading habits, attention, and understanding.

Ultimately, this project invites readers into an immersive, interactive encounter that mirrors Josef K.’s futile search for clarity. Through ergodic design, the publication embodies the complexities of digital reading, highlighting both its potential and its pitfalls, and reflecting on how we navigate meaning in the 21st century.
